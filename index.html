🏷️ Adjectives

Adjectives describe nouns — they tell us more about how something looks, feels, sounds, smells, or behaves.

ORDER in English (Opinion → Size → Age → Shape → Color → Origin → Material)

Opinion → Size → Age → Shape → Color → Origin → Material
Example: He is a handsome, short, young boy with curly black hair.

Position in a Sentence

Before a noun▼
She has a beautiful dress.
The old house creaked.
After linking verbs (be, seem, look, feel...)▼
The dress is beautiful.
He looks tired.
The music is loud.

💨 Adverbs of Manner

Adverbs of manner describe how an action happens. They usually modify verbs.

Examples: quickly, slowly, loudly, quietly, beautifully, carefully, badly, easily

How to Form Them

AdjectiveRuleAdverb
slow+ lyslowly
quick+ lyquickly
careful+ lycarefully
happyy → ilyhappily
gentlele → lygently
basicic → icallybasically
Irregular: good → well · fast → fast · hard → hard · late → late

Position in Sentence

He drives carefully. (after verb)
She painted the room beautifully. (after object)

🔮 Similes

Similes compare two different things using "like" or "as...as".

Authors use similes to create vivid mental images for the reader.

Examples from Emma's work

"He is as strong as an elephant." as...as
"The water was as cold as ice." as...as
"Her smile was like sunshine." like
"Flames came out of its mouth like an angry, erupting volcano!" like
"His heart beating like thunder" like

📝 Expanding Sentences

Take a basic sentence and make it more interesting by adding: at least one adjective, one adverb, and (if possible) a simile.
🐕 The dog ran.▼
The fluffy dog ran quickly, like a speeding bullet.
adjective: fluffy adverb: quickly simile: like a speeding bullet
🎤 The girl sang.▼
The talented girl sang beautifully, like a nightingale.
adjective: talented adverb: beautifully simile: like a nightingale
🚗 The car moved.▼
The old car moved slowly, like a tired turtle.
adjective: old adverb: slowly simile: like a tired turtle

Grammar & Descriptions

⏪ Past Simple

Use the Past Simple to talk about completed actions in the past.
FormStructureExample
✅ AffirmativeSubject + past verbThe boy turned to stone.
❌ Negativedid not / didn't + base verbThe boy did not turn to stone.
❓ QuestionDid + subject + base verb?Did the boy turn to stone?
⚠️ Watch out! When you use did, the main verb goes back to the infinitive (base form).
✗ Did the boy turned? → ✓ Did the boy turn?

From The Witches: Grandmamma's Secret

Fact: Ragnhild went away with the tall lady.
Negative: Ragnhild didn't go away with the tall lady.
Question: Did Ragnhild go away with the tall lady?
Fact: The girl grew feathers all over her body.
Negative: The girl didn't grow feathers all over her body.
Question: Did the girl grow feathers all over her body?

🖼️ Writing a Description

📋 Paragraph 1: Physical Description▼
Use basic features: hair color, eye color, skin tone, distinguishing features (freckles, scars, wrinkles, moles), clothing.

Descriptive adjectives: Include words for physical traits in detail (tall, short, muscular, slender, elegant).

Similes: Compare the character's features to something familiar.
Example: "His eyes are as blue as the ocean."
💭 Paragraph 2: Personality Traits▼
Describe behavior and actions: How does the character behave with others? Are they friendly, shy, loud, quiet, or brave?

Adverbs of manner: Include how the character behaves — kindly, cautiously, proudly, eagerly.

Adjectives for character traits: stubborn, compassionate, arrogant, generous, brave, caring, helpful.

📖 Example Description

PHYSICAL: The boy has short, light brown hair that is a little messy. His eyes are blue like the sky. His skin is fair, and he has a few small freckles on his nose. He is small and thin, so he looks younger than he is. He wears simple clothes like a T-shirt and shorts. His smile is as bright as the sun, and he looks kind and friendly.


PERSONALITY: The boy is brave and curious. He is friendly and talks kindly to others. He listens carefully and learns quickly. His mind is like a sponge because he remembers many things. Sometimes he feels scared, but he acts bravely and keeps going. He is also caring and helpful, and he behaves politely with other people.

adj: messy, small, thin, brave, curious adv: kindly, carefully, quickly, bravely, politely similes: like the sky · as bright as the sun · like a sponge

⚔️ Through the Magic Knife

Fantasy story · Colour-coded analysis

Colour key:

🔴 Main character on a quest 🔵 Descriptive setting 🟢 Build-up / dilemma hint 🟡 Portal between worlds 🟣 Magical characters

Cal was bored! It was the third day in a row that he had to stay indoors to look after his sick sister. And to top it all off, it was a beautiful day and he really wanted to be outside having an adventure.


Cal was sitting at the kitchen table as the bright sun shone through the window. On the kitchen bench lay a knife, its sharp blade gleaming. Cal noticed his reflection on the blade and thought, 'something's not quite right with this knife. I feel weird'.


Suddenly, a shadow of a dragon appeared on the blade. It looked so real! Bright flames flowed from its nostrils and its scaly wings stretched out as wide as his eyes could see. With his heart beating like thunder, Cal didn't dare to breathe. Just then, a gray cloud appeared in front of him. When the cloud disappeared, he was face-to-face with the large dragon, with fierce-looking eyes, standing in a dark, murky cave.


Stunned and petrified, Cal knew he had to escape. He escaped out of the cave and ran for his life! He could hear the terrifying dragon behind him, reaching on him. The furious dragon, with saliva drooling from its mouth, was ready to attack. Cal, in panic and desperation, searched for somewhere to hide.


The ferocious dragon got closer and closer and with its mouth open wide, it charged at great speed towards Cal. Flames came out of its mouth like an angry, erupting volcano!


Cal just about managed to avoid the dragon but knew he only had seconds to find somewhere to hide. He scurried around the corner to find himself back at the opening of the cave again. Suddenly, he found a little hollow that seemed just the right size for him to hide in. Crouching down and holding his breath for the second time that day, Cal jumped into it quickly. With outstretched wings and blazing nostrils, the dragon swooped down to follow Cal but the hole was way too small for it to get through.


Unexpectedly, Cal found himself back in his bright kitchen once again. 'Maybe being right here is just fine', Cal thought to himself, before noticing a strange burning smell and scorch marks all around the window frame...

🧙 The Witches — Roald Dahl

What is a Simile?
A simile compares two things using "like" or "as". Authors use similes to create vivid mental images for the reader.

Example: A witch's fingers are "like cold icicles" — you can imagine exactly how freezing and sharp they feel.

🦊 The Fox and the Crow

Y5 Reading Comprehension · Northlands

The story: A clever Fox sees a Crow on a tree holding cheese in her beak. The Fox uses flattery to make the Crow sing — and when she opens her beak, the cheese falls.
Q1: What did the animal on the tree have in its mouth?▼
✅ A cheese (in her beak)
Q2: Why does the Fox compliment the Crow?▼
The Fox compliments her because he wants the cheese. He uses flattery to make her open her beak.
Q3: What happens to the cheese when the Crow opens her beak?▼
When the Crow opens her beak to sing, the cheese falls straight into the Fox's open mouth. The Crow couldn't believe she had been tricked.
Q4: What lesson did you learn with this story?▼
I learn to not open your mouth because it falls the cheese. (Don't fall for flattery!)
Q7: Who is more responsible for the Crow losing the cheese?▼
The responsibility is the Crow's — she should not have believed the flattery and opened her beak.
